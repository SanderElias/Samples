# Patch Notes for Civilization: AI Edition

AI is changing the field as we speak. It's not going, it is _doing it right now!_ At the start of 2026, it had a grow-spurt. From a tool that was promising to be useful, it has become a tool that is actually more then useful. In this article, we will look at this. What has changed, and what does it mean for us?

## Bubble, or Boom?

Really, you tell me. For a long time I was in the bubble camp. I used it since its inception, and while it became steadily more impressive, it always felt like a toy to me. Not because it was unusable or bad, but because the quality of the output was not (by far) ready. It could to minor tasks, but as soon as the taks needed any real substance it would fail. 
Also the number of failures on tasks was high. And the mistakes where sometimes catastrophic. 

But then the start of 2026 came. And it was like a switch was flipped. Suddenly, the quality of the output was much higher. It could do much more complex tasks, and the number of failures went down drastically. It was like it had learned to learn. I was expecting this to happen, but not in this time frame. The scary thing is, it doesn't seem to be slowing down. We need to calibrate our expectations. It is not longer years away, it is here now. And the next thing is, months away, not years.

BTW, I know and understand that there was no _switch_, that flipped. There where many small improvements, but also a lot of things we had learned on how to use it. And tooling. I hate doing the buzzword thing, but, there is no real way around it here. Agent based prompting. Prompt engineering, MCP servers and clients, Skills, Memories, Gibberlink, OASF, ACP, OSI, the list goes on and on.
A lot of those snapped into place in the first months of 2026. I have heard people say; First it was community college level, but now its got it's its PhD!

Back to the premise, bubble or boom? I suspect it can be both. When does the bubble go boom? Or will it sizzle out? I don't know. But I do know that we have to deal with this. Ignoring is no longer a viable option. 

## What does it mean for us?

Well, something we as developers are used to. Change. Once more, we are in the middle of a paradigm shift. Although, this one is different, _very different_. Were the previous shifts I have seen were a small breeze, this one is a hurricane. There will be victims. How can we protect ourselves? I could only wish I had a good answer to that. But I don't. I can only give you some advice, and hope it helps.

Are there no silver linings? Yes, of coarse. First of, none of use is _that_ much ahead, we are all learning together. Secondly, in the current state, it is a tool that is good at solving the 'how', and very limited at the 'what' part. Notice how I left out the `why`. Why? because that is still the part that needs the most creativity. Now the scary bit. I said currently....
There is more. Even the most brilliant llm's are not able to do complex tasks by themselves. Also, the problem of hallucination is not going away. Notice the period there? Let me point you to the [OpenAI admits AI hallucinations are mathematically inevitable](https://www.computerworld.com/article/4059383/openai-admits-ai-hallucinations-are-mathematically-inevitable-not-just-engineering-flaws.html) article. Thats just one, and it is an article. But there are many studies that show things like this. Solid scientific research, is done. Not all off the outcomes are in favor of AI. But that is the point, we need to be aware of the limitations, and work with them.

Our jobs will be more about checking the output, and less about writing the code. There is this strange catch 22 in there. We need to learn to program, so we really understand what it takes

Our jobs are changing. I have been working with AI for a long time. I noticed this shift, and started to dive deeper into it. As I do when I want to learn something I did build my own version. Its all about the amount of dedicated  memory. Currently that is prohibitively expensive. There is no use in throwing numbers here. Those are aging so quickly, there is no point. That will also change, but not as fast as other things.
